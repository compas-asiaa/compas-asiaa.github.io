Brief History

The initiative was originally pioneered by Prof. Chi Yuan, in collaborations with Profs. I-Liang Chern (NTU), Tai-Ping Liu (Stanford University, Institute of Mathematics, AS) , Wei-Cheng Wang (NTHU) and Dr. Hsien Shang, for advancing and designing new algorithms, with intended applications to disk systems and star formation. Dr. Chien-Chang Yen advanced the first prototype of the Antares code, followed by Dr. Hsiang-Hsu Wang's various modifications and improvements to the original solver including implementation of nested-mesh for higher resolution at localized zones. After Prof. Chi Yuan passed away, Dr. Ronald Taam and the team applied the prototypical codes and algorithms to problems related to disk galaxies, escape of molecules in planetary atmospheres, followed by Dr. Hsien Shang for generalization to protoplanetary systems.

Currently, under supervision of Hsien Shang, the group is looking into generalization of the codes to physics and numerics in star and planet forming processes and high-energy accretion systems. Major updates are being implemented to improve the computational performance and numerical accuracy of the codes. These updates include new datastructures and algoritms, interfaces with Graphics Processing Unit (GPU) for faster computation and many others to overcome past limitations and keep the codes consistent with state-of-art computational astrophyscs.
